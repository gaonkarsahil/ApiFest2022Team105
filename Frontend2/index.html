Why are we doing this?

It’s been claimed in numerous reports that last year, during the early months of the pandemic, shelters saw record adoptions and that people who now are returning to the office and taking long-awaited vacations are surrendering their pandemic pets.

Many animals in animal shelters are not even listed for adoption. So to solve these issues we created this website

How this will help?

Through this website we are creating awareness about animal adoption and making the process easy.

This site will help many homeless animals to find a new house.

This website will help you to find your new best friend

By adoption you can Help in Control Animal Overpopulation

Fact vs Misconception about sheltered animals

Myths
Sheltered animals are wild

These animals are sick

They are abandoned by their owners due to bad behavior

Adoption is very complicated process

Facts
Most sheltered animals are already socialized and trained.

All animals at shelters are properly vaccinated and examined by doctors in regular basis

They are mostly animals whose owners are not able to afford them anymore or shifted to a new place and can’t take them.

It is as simple as filling one form and it cost way less then buying

Reality of homeless animals

Animals end up living on the street because humans no longer want them, they abandon and dump them.

Shelters euthanize animals.

Stray animals face abuse, homelessness, starvation, cruel people, predators, disease, illness, death.
